The 2025 Indian Premier League (IPL 18), branded as TATA IPL 2025, was
the 18th edition of the Indian Premier League. The tournament featured
10 teams competing in 74 matches. It began on 22 March and was held
across 13 venues before being suspended on 9 May due to the
India–Pakistan crisis. The matches resumed on 17 May across six venues,
with the final rescheduled to 3 June.

In the final, Royal Challengers Bengaluru defeated Punjab Kings by 6
runs to win their maiden IPL title after 18 years. The defending
champions, Kolkata Knight Riders, were eliminated in the league stage.

Sai Sudharsan scored the most runs (759), while Prasidh Krishna took the
most wickets (25), both representing Gujarat Titans. Suryakumar Yadav of
Mumbai Indians was awarded the Most Valuable Player of the tournament.

Background: The Indian Premier League is a professional Twenty20 cricket
league organized by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI). It
has been held annually since 2008. Kolkata Knight Riders were the
defending champions.

Format: Each team played group-stage matches followed by playoffs
consisting of Qualifier 1, Eliminator, and Qualifier 2.

Rule Changes: The IPL adopted the ICC Code of Conduct for T20
Internationals.

Schedule: The tournament began on 22 March with the opening match at Eden
Gardens.

Suspension and Rescheduling The tournament was suspended on 9 May and
resumed on 17 May across six venues.

Marketing: The Tata Group continued sponsorship under a ₹2,500 crore
deal.

Broadcasting: The tournament recorded over 253 million TV viewers and
1.37 billion digital views.

Teams and Auction: The auction was held in Jeddah. Rishabh Pant became
the most expensive player at ₹27 crore.

Venues: Matches were held across 13 stadiums, later reduced to six
venues.

League Stage Highlights The season saw multiple debuts and record
performances.

Playoffs Royal Challengers Bengaluru and Punjab Kings reached the final.

Final Royal Challengers Bengaluru won their first IPL title.

Statistics and Awards Most Runs: Sai Sudharsan (759) Most Wickets:
Prasidh Krishna (25) MVP: Suryakumar Yadav
