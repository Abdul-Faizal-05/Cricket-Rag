ORIGIN STORY

What is the IPL?

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is the world's most-watched Twenty20 cricket franchise tournament, launched in 2008 by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI). It revolutionized cricket by merging sport, entertainment, and commerce into a single spectacle.

Each season runs from March to May, featuring 10 city-based franchises competing in a round-robin format followed by playoffs. With billion-dollar broadcast rights and the world's best cricketers on the same pitch, the IPL is the second most-attended sporting league globally.

GAME FORMAT

T20 — The Shortest Format

Each IPL match is a Twenty20 (T20) contest — 20 overs per side, 120 deliveries, and roughly 3 hours of edge-of-seat cricket. This fast-paced format rewards power hitting, innovative bowling strategies, and split-second fielding decisions.

Teams can score between 120–230+ runs in a single innings. Batters aim to score at a strike rate above 150, while bowlers fight to contain the run flow using yorkers, slower balls, and bouncers. The last 5 overs — the "death overs" — often decide the game's outcome.

THE CHAMPIONS

IPL 2024 Champion: KKR

Kolkata Knight Riders (KKR) clinched their third IPL title in IPL 2024 with a dominant campaign. Led by Shreyas Iyer and powered by key performances from Venkatesh Iyer, Sunil Narine, and Mitchell Starc, KKR dismantled SunRisers Hyderabad in the final at Chennai.

SRH made history with the highest total ever in an IPL final, but KKR's bowling unit stood firm. Narine won the Player of the Tournament award for his all-round brilliance — scoring 488 runs and taking 17 wickets across the tournament.

RECORDS & GLORY

All-Time Records

Virat Kohli holds the record for the most runs in IPL history, having scored over 7,600 runs with multiple Orange Cap victories. CSK's MS Dhoni is the most iconic captain with 5 IPL titles and the most wins as a captain in IPL history.

Mumbai Indians (MI) remain the most successful franchise with 5 IPL titles. The highest individual score is 175* by Chris Gayle (RCB vs PWI, 2013), while Lasith Malinga holds the record for most wickets in IPL history with 170+ scalps.
